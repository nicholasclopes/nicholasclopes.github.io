About

I recently graduated with a Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University under the advisement of Dr. Sandra Boetcher. In grad school, my research interests included supercritical fluids, gas turbine combustor cooling, and phase-change materials for thermal energy storage. In 2022 I interned at Pratt & Whitney as an Aero-Thermal Engineer, and in 2024 I worked at the German Aerospace Center (DLR) in Cologne, Germany under an NSF grant. From 2021 through 2024 I was a teaching assistant for the General Chemistry I Lab at Embry-Riddle. During this time I gave occasional guest lectures in the Mechanical Engineering Department and personally mentored several undergraduate and graduate researchers. I am also an active member in several professional organizations including ASME and ASHRAE. From 2025 through my graduation in 2026 I conducted research full time in collaboration with Oak Ridge National Laboratory under a DOE grant.

This is my personal website and will serve as a landing place for my various academic and professional activities.
